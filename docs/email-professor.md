Hi [Professor's Name],

Hope you're doing well.

I've been working on a personal project over the past few months and thought you might find it interesting — or at least worth a look.

It's called **anima** — an open-source engine that simulates a person's inner mental world using multiple concurrent AI modules.

The idea is straightforward but a bit unusual: instead of building a chatbot that *responds*, I wanted something that *runs on its own* — simulating what's actually happening inside someone's mind between moments. Emotion drift, memory intrusion, inner voices, fragmented imagery, rumination — all running in parallel, every tick.

The emotion architecture draws on **OCC theory** (Ortony, Clore & Collins' cognitive appraisal model), so the emotional states aren't arbitrary — they're computed from goal relevance, attribution, and carry inertia across time steps.

There's also a browser-based visualization where the stream of consciousness floats across a dark screen, one character at a time.

**Two characters are currently modelled:**
- A 28-year-old designer in Beijing, sitting alone in a corridor after her proposal was rejected in front of the whole team
- Kobe Bryant — the night of January 25, 2020, after LeBron passed his scoring record, the night before the crash

The project is in Chinese (the prompts, the output), which is intentional — it's built specifically for the nuance and texture of Chinese inner experience. I think that constraint is part of what makes it interesting.

GitHub: **https://github.com/Kalexzhu/anima**
